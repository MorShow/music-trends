First graph: It is a timeline of your favourite album. The iTunes usually shares only sells information, so it's quite difficult to estimate the popularity of new music using this service. But there are some rankings and data libraries somehow trying to resolve this. For instance, kworb.net created a new way to assess the album/song's popularity based on the regional specifics. There are 100+ countries for which it is possible to estimate the popularity of the song. They were divided into four groups based on some consumption factors. Then these values are combined creating a final 'Points' feature creating this ranking. The figure represents this rating. You can choose the album from the list of the most popular ones and observe the tendencies.

* The values and albums are from 2014.

Second graph: The graph is showing how preferences differ from country to country. As a baseline was taken Slovakia. The results are shown on the top 10 most popular tracks for 2011–nowadays (based on aggregated sales values for this period).
